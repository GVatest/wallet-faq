Custodial vs. Self-custodial wallets

    View in Help Scout

    What is my TON Space?

    Your TON Space is a TON blockchain wallet that you completely control. You can send and receive NFTs, Jettons, and TON here.

    What is a Self-Custodial wallet?

    A Self-Custodial wallet describes when you, the user, are the only person with control of your funds, as you are the only party with the private key. 

    What is a Secret Recovery Phrase?

    When creating a TON Space, choose to either import an existing wallet or create a new one. 

    Creating a new wallet generates a Secret Recovery Phrase, a series of secret words. It's crucial to keep this phrase confidential. Anyone with access to it gains full control over your TON Space funds, including the ability to empty your accounts.

    ⚠️ Note: Keep the secrecy of your Secret Recovery Phrase.

    Where is my Secret Recovery Phrase?

    At any time, you can find your secret recovery phrase within your TON Space, by going to Settings (...) > TON Space > Tap to view phrase.

    ⚠️ Note: Keep the secrecy of your Secret Recovery Phrase.

    How can I import an existing wallet?

    You can import an existing wallet by clicking 'Import Existing' when creating a TON Space on a device. You will need to type in the Secret Recovery Phrase for the TON wallet that you want to import.

    How can I recover a lost wallet?

    During TON Space creation, you can provide your email for wallet recovery via email and Telegram accounts. If not, you can recover using your Secret Recovery Phrase. Keep your Secret Recovery Phrase safe for potential TON Space recovery.

    ⚠️ Note: If you do not have your Secret Recovery Phrase or email recovery you can lose your funds.

    How does wallet recovery work on a technical level?

    If you enable TON Space recovery, your wallet's private key is divided into two parts: one stored with Telegram, linked to your Telegram account, and the other stored with Wallet, linked to your email.

    To recover your TON Space, both parts are combined after verifying your access to your Telegram account and the provided email. Neither Telegram nor Wallet have full access to your private key. The two parts of your key are only recombined directly on the new device once restore is complete.

    ⚠️ Note: If you lose access to your Telegram account or your email, you will need your Secret Recovery Phrase to recover your funds.

    What does TON Space do with my seed phrase and private key?

    Sensitive information like seed phrases and private keys are only stored on the user device they were inputted. They are never sent to any server. Neither Wallet nor Telegram, have access to your seed phrase. If you lose your seed phrase, neither Wallet nor Telegram can help you recover it.

    In the future, TON Space will have an optional feature where users can opt-in to a secure recovery system. In this system, your seed phrase is encrypted on your device, it then shares the encryption key with Wallet and encryption contents with Telegram Cloud Storage.

    Contents within Telegram Cloud Storage can only be accessed by the user while logged into Telegram, Telegram bots cannot access this information by server to server communication. This means that the only location where both the encryption key and contents exist are on the user’s device. Wallet only has a random key, and Telegram only has an encrypted string, meaning neither Wallet nor Telegram ever have access to your seed phrase. Only the user will ever have access.

    How many wallets can I import?

    As for now, you can import one wallet across as many devices as you would like.

    Will my self-custodial wallet / TON Space be visible from other devices?

    Since your private key is only kept locally on each device, you will need to manually import your Secret Recovery Phrase to each new device you would like to access your TON Space from. At this time, if you have a TON Space logged in on any device, you must re-use that TON Space address and Secret Recovery Phrase when logging in.

    ⚠️ Note: It is not currently possible to use multiple TON addresses in a TON Space.
